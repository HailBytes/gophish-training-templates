Why ITSM Attacks Are High-Value

IT Service Management platforms like ServiceNow, Jira Service Management, and Freshservice manage change requests, access approvals, and incident escalations. Attackers target these because:

  • Approval authority: Approving a fake ticket can grant real system access
  • SLA pressure: "Breach in 2 hours" forces hasty decisions
  • Technical legitimacy: IT jargon (CHG numbers, priority levels) makes emails convincing
  • Credential theft: Fake approval portals capture ITSM login credentials
  • Trust chains: Escalated tickets appear to have already been reviewed by others

Common ITSM Attack Patterns

  • Emergency change requests: "Critical Priority" tickets with tight deadlines demanding immediate approval
  • Auto-approve threats: "Access will be granted automatically if you don't respond" — real ITSM systems don't work this way
  • Credential theft via fake portals: The "Review & Approve" link leads to a fake login page
  • Ticket number spoofing: Realistic-looking ticket numbers (CHG0042891) increase believability
  • Escalation from fictional colleagues: "Requested by J. Martinez, Ops Team" adds fake legitimacy

Red Flags in ITSM Emails

  • Auto-approve deadlines: Real ITSM systems expire tickets — they don't automatically grant access
  • Sender domain: Real ServiceNow notifications come from your company's instance domain
  • Unfamiliar requesters: Verify ticket requesters through internal directories, not the email
  • Unsolicited approvals: If you weren't expecting to be an approver for something, verify out-of-band
  • Extreme urgency + high-risk action: "Emergency production DB access" requiring immediate approval is a red flag

Safe ITSM Practices

  1. Log into ServiceNow directly using your bookmarked URL — never approve tickets via email links alone
  2. Verify the requester through your internal directory before approving high-risk changes
  3. Escalate unusual requests — if a change request seems suspicious, contact your change advisory board or manager
  4. Know your approval scope: If a ticket falls outside your normal approval authority, reject and reassign
  5. Report suspicious ITSM emails to IT Security — especially those claiming SLA breaches for access requests
