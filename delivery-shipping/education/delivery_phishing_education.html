📦 About Delivery & Shipping Phishing Attacks

You received a simulated phishing email that appeared to be from a shipping company (DHL, FedEx, UPS, etc.). These attacks are extremely common because:

  • Universal Appeal: Everyone receives packages
  • Urgency: "Your package needs immediate attention"
  • Trust: We trust legitimate shipping companies
  • Expectation: We often expect packages we've forgotten about

🚨 Red Flags to Watch For

In the email you received, these were warning signs:

  • 📧 Unexpected delivery: You weren't expecting a package
  • 🔗 Suspicious links: URL doesn't match the real company
  • ⚡ Urgency pressure: "Verify immediately or lose your package"
  • 📝 Poor grammar/spelling: Professional companies proofread
  • 🎯 Generic information: Real tracking emails have specific details
  • 📨 Sender mismatch: Email doesn't come from official domain

✅ How to Verify Legitimate Shipping Emails

  1. Check the sender: Real emails come from @dhl.com, @fedex.com, @ups.com
  2. Log in separately: Go directly to the shipping company's website
  3. Use tracking numbers: Enter tracking numbers on the official website
  4. Look for specifics: Real emails have detailed tracking history
  5. Verify your orders: Check your recent online purchases
  6. Contact the company: Call customer service if unsure

🎯 What Attackers Want

Delivery phishing attacks typically aim to steal:
  • Login credentials (username/password)
  • Personal information (address, phone, SSN)
  • Credit card and payment information
  • Corporate network access

🛡️ Protecting Yourself Going Forward

Remember the "STOP, LOOK, THINK" method:

  • 🛑 STOP: Don't immediately click links or download attachments
  • 👀 LOOK: Examine the sender, URL, and content carefully
  • 🤔 THINK: Does this make sense? Am I expecting this?

🆘 What If You Think You've Been Compromised?

If you entered credentials or personal information:

  1. Change your passwords immediately
  2. Enable two-factor authentication
  3. Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
  4. Report the incident to IT Security
  5. Consider credit monitoring if financial info was shared
