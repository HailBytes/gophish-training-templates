Retail / Loyalty Phishing Detected!

You clicked on a simulated retail or loyalty program phishing email.
Fake rewards expiration notices and exclusive offers are designed to create FOMO (fear of missing out) that bypasses careful thinking.

Why Retail Phishing Is Common

Most people have 5-20 loyalty and rewards accounts. The high volume of legitimate retail emails makes fraudulent ones easy to miss. Key tactics include:

  • Points expiration scarcity: "Your 4,200 points expire tomorrow"
  • Exclusive offer urgency: "24-hour flash sale — click to unlock"
  • Order notification spoofing: Fake shipping updates for orders you didn't place (or did place)
  • Account security alerts: "Unauthorized redemption detected on your account"
  • Gift card scams: Fake gift card balances with instructions to "transfer" them

Common Retail Attack Patterns

  • Points expiration warnings: Fake urgency about losing valuable rewards
  • Order confirmation fraud: "Your $347 Amazon order is confirmed — click to cancel if you didn't order"
  • Loyalty account compromise alerts: Fake security warnings that steal credentials
  • Refund/rebate notifications: "Your refund of $89 is waiting — click to claim"
  • Survey scams: "Complete this quick survey for a $50 gift card" — credential or payment theft

Red Flags

  • Unexpected expiration notices: Check your account directly — real points expiration is shown in your account dashboard
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers: "Exclusive 90% off for you specifically" is a manipulation tactic
  • Order confirmations for things you didn't buy: Log into the retailer directly to check your order history
  • Sender domain not matching the brand: amazon-orders.net instead of amazon.com
  • Login required to claim rewards: Navigate directly to the retailer's website instead of clicking the email link

Protecting Your Retail Accounts

  1. Log into retail accounts directly to check points balances — don't trust email notifications
  2. Use unique passwords for retail/loyalty accounts — credential stuffing is common in retail phishing
  3. Enable 2FA on retail accounts that hold payment information or significant points value
  4. Be skeptical of urgency — if an offer expires in 24 hours, check the brand's website directly
  5. Check order status in the app — don't use order confirmation email links to dispute charges
