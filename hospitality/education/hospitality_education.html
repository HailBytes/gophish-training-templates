Hotel, airline, and travel phishing exploits the excitement and anxiety of travel plans to steal credentials and payment information.

Why Travel Phishing Works

Business travelers receive legitimate hotel and travel emails constantly. Attackers blend into this noise with fake:

  • Booking confirmations with slightly wrong details
  • Check-in/check-out reminders requesting credit card updates
  • Loyalty program account alerts
  • Corporate travel policy change notifications
  • Last-minute price change or cancellation notices

Common Travel Attack Patterns

  • Fake reservation confirmations: Sent when you're likely traveling — "confirm payment method for your upcoming stay"
  • Loyalty account security alerts: "Your Marriott/Hilton/IHG points have been redeemed — verify now"
  • Corporate travel portal credential theft: Fake Concur, Egencia, or Amex GBT login pages
  • Booking modification scams: "Your reservation requires action due to a policy change"
  • WiFi credential harvesting: Fake hotel WiFi login pages that capture credentials

Red Flags in Travel Emails

  • Unexpected reservation emails: If you didn't book it, contact the hotel/airline directly
  • Credit card update requests: Real hotels don't request payment re-entry via email
  • Loyalty alert + immediate action: Check your loyalty account directly via the brand's app
  • Sender domain mismatch: hilton-reservations.com instead of hilton.com
  • Suspicious check-in links: Digital key/check-in is done through the official brand app

Safe Travel Booking Habits

  1. Use official brand apps for check-in, loyalty points, and reservation management
  2. Verify reservations by logging directly into the booking platform (hotel website, Expedia, etc.)
  3. Use virtual credit cards for travel bookings when possible to limit exposure
  4. Call the hotel directly if you receive unexpected reservation or payment emails
  5. Use VPN on hotel WiFi and avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public networks
