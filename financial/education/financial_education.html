üè¶ Understanding Financial Phishing

Financial phishing attacks target your money, banking credentials, and financial accounts. They're among the most dangerous because:

  • Direct Financial Loss: Immediate access to your money
  • Identity Theft: Use your information for loans, credit cards
  • Authority Mimicry: Impersonate banks, government agencies
  • Fear Tactics: "Your account will be closed" urgency

üîí Protecting Your Financial Information

Financial Security Best Practices:

  1. Never click links in financial emails - Always log in directly to your bank's website
  2. Verify independently - Call your bank using the number on your card or statement
  3. Check your accounts regularly - Monitor for unauthorized transactions
  4. Use strong, unique passwords - Enable two-factor authentication
  5. Be skeptical of urgency - Real banks don't operate on emergency timelines
  6. Know your institutions - Banks know your name, account details, recent activity

üÜò If You've Been Compromised

Immediate Steps if You Clicked or Shared Information:

  1. Contact your bank immediately - Report potential fraud
  2. Change all banking passwords - Use new, strong passwords
  3. Monitor your accounts - Check for unauthorized transactions
  4. Enable account alerts - Get notified of all account activity
  5. Check your credit report - Look for unauthorized accounts
  6. Consider a credit freeze - Prevent new accounts from being opened
  7. Report to authorities - File reports with FTC and local police
