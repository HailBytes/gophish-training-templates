Why Utility Phishing Works

Utility companies (electric, gas, water, internet) have enormous leverage over our daily lives. Attackers exploit this with:

  • Disconnection threats: "Pay now or your service will be interrupted"
  • Credit/refund notices: "You have an outstanding credit — claim it now"
  • Smart meter/account update requirements: Technical-sounding reasons requiring credential re-verification
  • Suspicious usage alerts: Fear about abnormally high bills or unauthorized usage

Common Utility Attack Patterns

  • Overdue bill notices: "Your account is past due — pay now to avoid service interruption"
  • Credit/refund claims: "Power outage credit of $67.40 is available — click to claim"
  • Account portal "verification" requests: Fake login pages collecting utility account credentials (which often also contain payment info)
  • Smart meter enrollment: "Your meter upgrade requires account re-registration"
  • Payment plan offers: "Enroll in a payment plan before disconnection"

Red Flags

  • Disconnection threat via email only: Real utility companies send physical notices and follow a regulated disconnection process
  • Unusual refund or credit: Check your account directly — real credits appear in your account statement
  • Payment via non-standard method: Requests for gift cards, wire transfers, or cryptocurrency for utility payments are always scams
  • Sender domain mismatch: pge-billing.net instead of pge.com
  • Urgency + same-day payment demands: Real utilities give advance notice with regulated timelines

Safe Utility Account Practices

  1. Pay utility bills through official apps or by typing the URL directly — bookmark your utility company's payment portal
  2. Call the number on your physical bill if you receive a disconnection notice via email
  3. Never pay utilities with gift cards, wire transfers, or cryptocurrency — these are guaranteed scams
  4. Check credits/refunds in your account directly — log in via your bookmark, not an email link
  5. Set up autopay through official channels to reduce exposure to fake payment notices
